    How the Day Starts

    Like everyday everywhere, it starts with pouring a cup of Joe...

    We spend time looking at the map for ebikes and scooters ('vehicles') that indicate a need for service.

      That need for service occurs:
    1. when we have not looked at and touched them for at least 48 hours,
    2. it has a low state of charge,
    3. it has been taken out of the geocached service area
    4. customer reported problems with the vehicle
    5. complaints about vehicles on private property

    Loading the van

    With our day's list of tasks in hand we load the vans with the essentials: Spare batteries, bike tools, bike pump, and personal stuff.

    We also load the vans with any scooters, ebikes, or pedal bikes that are ready go back into the field. Scooters with low state of charge are retrieved from the field and brought back to charge overnight. Once charged, we redeply them to the field. ebikes and pedal bikes only get brought in when they need repair that cannot be accomodated in the field.

    A normal day

    A normal day is just driving to each vehicle, doing what needs to done to it: Scanning, servicing, picing up for charge, replacing a battery, fixing a grip, etc. When the list of things to do are done, the day in the field is essetialy done. Not too much excitement here other than chatting with the people on the street.

    An eventful trip of picking up ebikes and scooters

    It was to be yet another day hunting down ebikes and scooters that people had used and left in those less than desirable places. I was in route to one when about a block up I saw someone on one of our ebikes. Curiosity got the better of me and I wanted to see if the app would show it as an 'Active Trip', so I followed. I pulled up the map screen on the app and selected "Active Trips", but what do you think I saw? Correct. I did not see an active trip - at all, anywhere. There had to be one... if this guy was actually on an active trip. So I pursued.

    He was pedaling the bike and not going very fast. His speed was slow and labored, as if the electric assist was not working. He turned his head and saw that I was following, but could not seem to go any faster. One corner after another he would take in a feeble attempt to elude me. I just followed, keeping my distance, knowing that at some point he would need to stop.

    I pulled up the camera app on my phone and made ready to take a short video. I closed the distance between us so I could get a better recording and a clear image of who this was. He felt my presence and turned to look at me. After noticing that I was recording him, he stopped at the next corner of a busy intersection at stop sign. I pulled the van next to him, leaving it in drive just in case I needed a speedy getaway.

    I rolled the window down about halfway and said, "That bike belongs to RIDE KC. You can either get off and walk away, or I can call the police. Which do you want me to do?" After a feeble attempt to argue his case that it was his bike, I pointed out to him that it still had our name on it. Then I repeated, "You can either get off and walk away, or I can call the police. Which do you want me to do?" He shook his head, laid the bike on the ground, and walked away. After he was out of sight, I retrieved the stolen and broken bike and put it in my van.

    Then, off I went back to my origin mission to collect another ebike...

    At the end of the day

    The days work is done, time to return to the shop. We unload the vans. Tools get stored in the shop. Batteries get put on the chargers to charge overnight. Scooters brought in for low state of charge get put on charge overnight. Vehicles brought in for repair are put in 'repair' status and labled with what needs to get fixed. Vans get parked.

    The day is done and everything is ready for the next day to start it all over again.
